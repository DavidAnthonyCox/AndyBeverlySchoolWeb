Welcome


This course is required to be eligible to take the Florida 2-20 General Lines Agent State Examination. In addition to the course, you should be familiar with the study manual and any handouts we give you (printed or online) that say something to the effect of "this information will be on the exam." If we draw attention to it, it is definitely important! You also received a registration email that contains important details.

Structure of the Course

  1. Your instructor will communicate with you regularly and make an effort to reach out when it looks like you need help. You'll need to schedule time to study and assimilate information. We encourage you to start as soon as possible, and try the quizzes, forum, and other course tools.
  2. There is a course-wide FORUM where you can ask and answer questions. If you answer a question, do your best to verify your answer. A teacher will read the questions and clarify any points made, and will answer questions.
  3. Discussion is invited and expected. Talking or posting about the concepts we cover in this course helps everyone learn. You can always reach a teacher for clarification too -- by messaging through this site, by phone, etc.
  4. Each section consists of a Reading Assignment, Basic Data, and a Self Test. These correspond to the Basic Data and Self Test used in our live classrooms. Once you finish the Reading and Basic Data, go on to the Self Test to find out how much of the material you understood. You can go back to Basic Data as many times as needed. If there is still something you don't understand, ask about it in the forum.
  5. You need to score 70% or higher on the quiz for each section before you can access the next section (Basic Data and Quiz). Some sections may have 2 or more quizzes. Quizzes are risk-free; use them to help you learn the material and test your comprehension.
  6. At the end of the course there is an exam, which can be found in the last section. You can make two attempts to get a passing score of 70%, so make sure you know the material in each section before taking it! The exam also has a time limit, and you will need to stay connected to the Internet for the entire exam.


Qualifying for licensure
After you pass this 200 hour course, we will upload your certificate to the Division of Agents and Agency Services. Next you will need to sign up for the state exam, and be fingerprinted. For a brief guide to getting a license, see: http://andybeverly.com/help/getting-license/

Retaking this course
You will have access to this course content for 120 days after your registration date. Please be familiar with our policies on delaying, pausing, and extending your course access here. If you wait to communicate with us until after the course has expired, paying to re-register for the course may be your only option.

Learning styles

You might notice that we support multiple ways of learning the material. If you are having trouble with something, try to think about how we can help you understand it better. Some people learn best by reading, others by hearing, others by discussing with fellow students or asking questions, and still others by diagrams or pictures. So as you progress, pay attention to how you learn. Don't be shy about starting a conversation with classmates, or asking your instructor to present something another way.

If you have any questions other than the course content please contact us at info@andybeverly.com.

Exam

You can make two attempts to get a passing score of 70%, so make sure you know the material in each section before taking it! There are 150 questions. The exam also has a "2 hour 30 minute" time limit, and you will need to stay connected to the Internet for the entire exam. When you take the state exam, it will have 165 questions (150 questions plus 15 "pre-test" questions) and a 2 hour 45 minute limit.

The reason you have two attempts is in case you are disconnected and can not finish the exam. Therefore, do not waste an attempt.

If you can not pass in two attempts, regardless of the reason, you will need to speak with your teacher and do remedial work. That may include registering and paying for an exam prep, or other forms of help.

There is a 48 hour waiting period between your 1st and 2nd exam attempt. Further attempts, if approved, will have at least a 1 week waiting period. If your course expires before you have taken your exam attempts, you will need to apply for a course extension. See: http://andybeverly.com/catalog/access-extension/

The exam takes up to 2 hour 30 minute, so start it well before the course expiration. Otherwise your access may expire during the exam, wasting an exam attempt.
